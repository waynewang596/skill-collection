# Style Contract: GW Observational Results Paper

## Page Composition and Layout

- **Format**: Two-column academic journal layout (Physical Review X style)
- **Column width**: ~8.5 cm per column with ~0.8 cm gutter
- **Margins**: ~2 cm top/bottom, ~1.5 cm sides
- **Text density**: High -- typical 45-50 lines per column, small font
- **Text alignment**: Fully justified
- **Paragraph indentation**: First-line indent (no extra spacing between paragraphs)
- **Line spacing**: Single spacing within paragraphs
- **First page**: Title + author + abstract + DOI + subject areas + start of
  Introduction ALL on page 1. NO separate title page or abstract page.

## Typography System

- **Body text**: Serif font (Times-based, e.g., Times-Roman), ~10 pt, fully justified
- **Section headings (Level 1)**: Small caps, bold, numbered with Roman numerals.
  Format: "I. INTRODUCTION", "II. METHODS", "III. PROPERTIES INFERRED ...",
  "IV. LIMITS ON POSTMERGER SIGNAL", "V. CONCLUSIONS"
- **Section headings (Level 2)**: Bold, title case. Format: "A. Bayesian method",
  "B. Data", "C. Waveform models for binary neutron stars", "D. Tidal parameters"
- **Abstract**: Slightly smaller font or same as body, single paragraph, full width
  or spanning both columns
- **Math text**: Italic serif for variables, upright for operators
- **Equations**: Centered, numbered with Arabic numerals in parentheses on right
  margin: (1), (2), (3), ...
- **Figure captions**: Preceded by "FIG. N." in all caps/bold, followed by
  description text. Placed below the figure.
- **Table captions**: Preceded by "TABLE N." in all caps, followed by detailed
  description including error notation legend. Placed above the table.

## Running Headers and Footers

- **Page 1 header**: "PHYSICAL REVIEW X 9, 011001 (2019)" (centered, small caps)
- **Even pages (left header)**: Shortened article title in small caps.
  Example: "PROPERTIES OF THE BINARY NEUTRON STAR MERGER ..."
- **Odd pages (right header)**: Journal citation in small caps.
  Example: "PHYS. REV. X 9, 011001 (2019)"
- **Page numbers**: Bottom center. Format: "011001-N" where N is the sequential
  page number starting from 1.
- **Page 1 footer only**: "2160-3308/19/9(1)/011001(32)" on left,
  "011001-1" center, "Published by the American Physical Society" on right

## Color Palette

- **Text**: Black on white
- **Figures**: Multi-color for different waveform models (consistent palette)
  - TaylorF2: red/pink
  - PhenomPNRT: blue
  - PhenomDNRT: teal/cyan
  - SEOBNRT: green
  - Prior distributions: light gray or yellow
  - Credible regions: shaded colored contours with solid/dashed boundary lines
  - EOS curves: black lines with labels
- **Links/references**: Blue hyperlinks (in PDF output)

## Visual Treatment of Tables

- **Style**: Booktabs (horizontal rules only, no vertical lines)
- **Top rule**: Thick (2pt)
- **Header separator**: Thin (0.5pt)
- **Bottom rule**: Thick (2pt)
- **Internal separators**: None or minimal (thin between major row groups)
- **Column alignment**: Left for text, right for numbers
- **Math in tables**: Italic symbols with units in upright text
- **Table caption**: Placed above the table, preceded by "TABLE N." in bold

## Figure Placement and Sizing

- **Placement**: Floating, typically at top of column or page, interrupting text
- **Width**: Either single-column (~8.5 cm) or full-page width (~17.5 cm) for
  wide/multi-panel figures
- **Multi-panel figures**: Arranged vertically (stacked panels) for comparisons
  across prior choices (high-spin vs low-spin)
- **White space**: Minimal around figures; captions immediately below
- **Color bar**: Included when figures show probability density (e.g., spin plots)

## Layout Rhythm

- Sections flow continuously without explicit page breaks
- Figures interrupt the text flow at natural paragraph breaks
- Each major result section follows: introductory text -> figure reference in
  text -> figure with caption -> detailed numerical discussion -> model comparison
- Tables placed near their first text reference
- Equations placed inline with the text that references them, numbered sequentially

## Reference Citation Style

- Numbered citations in square brackets: [3], [38,39], [101-103]
- Citations placed after punctuation (outside the period)
- Multiple citations grouped and sorted: [3,38-40]
- Reference list at end of document, numbered sequentially
- Typical count: 150-200 references for a major GW results paper

## Author List Formatting

- **First page**: "B. P. Abbott et al." with collaboration below:
  "(LIGO Scientific Collaboration and Virgo Collaboration)"
- **Footnote on first page**: "Full author list given at the end of the article."
- **End of paper**: Full author list with extensive affiliations spanning 10+ pages
- Each author with superscript affiliation markers
- Affiliations listed as numbered institutions
